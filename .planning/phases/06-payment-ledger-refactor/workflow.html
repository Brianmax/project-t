Phase 06 — Payment Ledger Workflow

Single payment kind. Tenant credit balance. FIFO application. Visual companion to .planning/phases/06-payment-ledger-refactor/06-PLAN.md.

The model in one paragraph

Each contract has a running ledger. Payments add to it (refunds subtract). Issued receipts consume it in FIFO order — oldest month first. A receipt becomes paid when its totalDue has been fully covered by accumulated credit; otherwise it stays unpaid. Surplus credit not consumed by any receipt is the tenant's positive balance, which automatically applies to future receipts. The contract's advancePayment and guaranteeDeposit fields are deposits — they live on the contract and never flow through this ledger.

Three scenarios as ledger walks

Scenario 1 — exact payment

Tenant has one receipt outstanding (S/ 1400) and pays S/ 1400.

Initial: totalPaid=0 totalBilled=1400 balance=-1400 Action: record Payment(amount=1400) Recalc: running=1400 receipt[2026-05]: due=1400 applied=1400 remaining=0 → PAID creditRemaining=0 Final: balance=0 receipt PAID

Scenario 2 — partial payment

Same receipt (S/ 1400). Tenant pays S/ 700.

Initial: totalPaid=0 totalBilled=1400 balance=-1400 Action: record Payment(amount=700) Recalc: running=700 receipt[2026-05]: due=1400 applied=700 remaining=700 → UNPAID creditRemaining=0 Final: balance=-700 receipt UNPAID (700 of 1400 covered)

The receipt's stored totalPayments = 700, balance = −700 after the projector runs. The receipt stays unpaid; no "partial" status exists.

Scenario 3 — overpayment creates credit

Same receipt (S/ 1400). Tenant pays S/ 1500. Then, next month, a new S/ 1400 receipt is issued and the tenant pays only S/ 1300.

Step A — overpayment Initial: totalPaid=0 totalBilled=1400 balance=-1400 Action: record Payment(amount=1500) Recalc: running=1500 receipt[2026-05]: due=1400 applied=1400 remaining=0 → PAID creditRemaining=100 After A: balance=+100 receipt PAID, surplus credit 100 Step B — next month, partial covers via credit Issue: receipt[2026-06]: due=1400 Initial: totalPaid=1500 totalBilled=2800 balance=-1300 Action: record Payment(amount=1300) Recalc: running=2800 receipt[2026-05]: applied=1400 → PAID receipt[2026-06]: applied=1400 → PAID (1300 fresh + 100 carry) creditRemaining=0 Final: balance=0 both receipts PAID

FIFO application order

When credit is applied, the receipt list is walked oldest-first: ORDER BY year ASC, month ASC, createdAt ASC. The operator does not choose which receipt a payment lands on; the ledger does it deterministically.

running = 2000
receipt 2026-03 · due 1400 · applied 1400 · paid
→
running = 600
receipt 2026-04 · due 1400 · applied 600 · unpaid
→
running = 0
receipt 2026-05 · due 1400 · applied 0 · unpaid

A tenant who has S/ 2000 in payments and three S/ 1400 receipts pays March in full, partially covers April, and contributes nothing to May. No partial status — the receipt is either fully covered or it is unpaid.

What deletion does

When a payment is deleted, recalculate() runs again with the new payment set. Receipts that were only paid because of that payment flip back to unpaid, and their paidAt / paidBy audit columns are cleared. This is the same deterministic walk — nothing about deletion is special-cased.

Before: payments = [P1=1500] receipt[2026-05] PAID, credit=100 Action: delete P1 Recalc: running=0 receipt[2026-05]: applied=0 remaining=1400 → UNPAID paidAt = NULL, paidBy = NULL After: balance=-1400 receipt UNPAID

Operator mental model (one screen for the team)

  • Recording a payment is the only way to mark a receipt as paid. No status toggle.
  • The system decides where the money goes: oldest unpaid receipt first, surplus becomes credit.
  • Overpayment is safe: extra credit sits on the contract and applies to the next month automatically.
  • Refunds are negative payments with the same form, no special button.
  • Deleting a payment is honest: receipts that were only paid because of it become unpaid again. The audit trail (paidAt / paidBy) is cleared on the same write.
  • Deposits (anticipo, garantía) stay on the contract row — they are not part of the running balance.
